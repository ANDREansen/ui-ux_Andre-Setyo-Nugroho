+ Step by step : create goals
1) Understand the background :
	a) Ask your stakeholder what they want to learn
	b) Collect and study the available data first 
2) Write down the goal
	a) try to be concise and narrow down the goals to a specific aspect of user behavior /attitude
+ Step by step : define participant criteria
Prefer extreme users     

+ Step by step : define number of participants needed
The best result come from testing no more than 5 users and running as many small test as you can afford

+ Step by step : Develop research guideline 
1) Opening : Ice breaking, give more context
	a) Getting to know the participant 
	b) General behavior questions
	c) Usabili testing topic relates questions

2) Usabilit tetsing task
3) Closing : Rating and feedback
	a) Ask the participant to rate the experience
	b) Likes, Disklikes & Feedback to improve the experience

1) Start from the most generic thing that the participant would do an the first page before moving on to the specific task

	a) Task 2 : ( once the participant land on the booking pagasE) from page assume you want to buy a train ticket with following details:

+ Step by step : dos and dont during the testing
1) Tell the participants that youre testing your desian, not them, this is not an exam
2) Observer and take notes on things that participant do on your desain, A screen recordes would help you to recall the journey later
3) Allow the participant to explore first.answer the questions after the session finished
4) Encourage the participant to speak out loud
5) Ask the follow up questin after the pasticip[ant finished the tas
6) Do not ask leading question or express the non-neutral statement

# Explanation
A/B Testing, also known as split testing. refers to a randomized experimentation process wherein two or more versions of variable (web page, page element, etc.)

# Why u should consider A/B Tetsing?

- Get better ROI from existing traffic
- Reduce bounce rate
- Redesign website to increase future business gains

# A/B Tetsing statictical approach
1) Frequentist Approach
- Frequentist statistic follow the probability as log term frequency definition of probability
- In this approach, you only use data dorm your current experiment. The frequentist solustion is to cinduct test and draw conclusion.
- Give an estimated mean (and standard deviation) of sample where A beats B but completely ignores the cases when B beats A
- Requires the test to run for a set period to get correct data from it but cant figure out how close or far A and B actually are. it fails 
to tell you the probability of a beating b
2) Bayesian Approch
- Byesian statistics follow the nations of probability as degree of belief logical probability
- In this approach, you use your prior knowledge from the previous experiment and try to incorporate that information into your current data. The bayesian solution is 
to use existing data to draw conclusion
- It takes into account the possibility of A beating B and also calculates the range of the improvement you can expect
- Gives you more control over testing. You can now plan batter, have a more accurate reasin to end test, and get into the nitty-gritty of how close or fro apart A
and B are.
